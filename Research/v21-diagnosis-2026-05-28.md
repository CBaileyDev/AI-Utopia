## 0. Executive summary

The v21 training run is **not failing for one reason — it's failing for four reasons stacked together**. Three parallel specialist agents (deep-RL training, Minecraft env, reward design) independently inspected the codebase and converged on the same root-cause set:

1. **Broken PPO numerics** — gradient norm at iter 1 was **643× the `grad_clip=1.0` ceiling**; `vf_clip_param=10.0` is fatally undersized vs. episode returns that hit **−633.86** at iter 3; adaptive KL is in a monotonic runaway (0.30 → 0.45 → 0.68 → 1.01 over 5 iters because every other iter is NaN); the actor head has **no `log_std` clamp**, so the Gaussian heads drive variance toward infinity (`entropy = 198.7` at iter 1).
2. **Exploit penalty stack is unbounded** — `NOOP_SKILL_SPAM` levies `0.2 × recent_fails` *per tick*; a stuck agent can rack up `−2.0/tick`, which over 300 ticks = **−600** reward. This is the source of the iter-3 outlier that nuked the value function.
3. **`action_mask` is in the obs space but never applied to the logits.** All 6 skills sample uniformly regardless of whether targets are in range — directly producing the IMMEDIATE_FAILUREs that drive (2).
4. **JVM-side entity leak + G1 GC growth pauses** — `WorldOps.resetEpisode` clears blocks but **does not kill item entities or XP orbs** that drop outside the reset volume. Linear accumulation across episodes is what manifests as the "step time grows 1.5s → 12s over 5–10 iters" symptom.

The fixes are inexpensive and low-risk. The biggest question — **build a custom Python/JAX sim?** — gets a **strong YES** from all three agents for M1B specifically, with caveats (see §3).

## 2. Convergent diagnosis from the three agents

### 2.1 PPO numerics (`src/aiutopia/train/config.py:92–107`)

| Hyperparameter | Current | Recommended | Why |
|---|---|---|---|
| `grad_clip` | **1.0** | **10.0** | iter-1 pre-clip norm = 643; clip-fraction ~1.0/643 = 0.15% → optimizer is silently no-op'ing |
| `vf_clip_param` | **10.0** | **200.0** or `None` | returns range −634 → +21; clip-window 20 means value head gets ~0 gradient on the part that matters |
| `kl_coeff` | 0.2 adaptive | **0.0 (clip-only PPO)** *or* 0.5 fixed | adaptive ramped 0.3→1.0 in 5 iters; NaN iters don't reset it; classic runaway |
| `entropy_coeff` | 0.01 | **0.001** | combined with unclamped Gaussian heads → entropy=198.7 (~40× expected); rewards log_std → ∞ |
| `lr` | 3.0e-4 | 1.0e-4 | aggressive for batch=256 + LSTM truncated BPTT |
| Actor head `log_std` clamp | **missing** | clamp to `[-5.0, 2.0]` | direct cause of entropy explosion → NaN |

### 2.2 Action mask not applied (`src/aiutopia/rl_module/actor_head.py`)

`build_role_observation_space` ships a full `action_mask` (`src/aiutopia/env/spaces.py:41`). `actor_head.py` does **not reference it** — grep returns zero hits. Add `logits = logits + (1 - mask) * -1e9` before `TorchMultiDistribution.from_logits`. This is the highest-leverage single change for reducing IMMEDIATE_FAILURE rates and the cascading exploit penalties they trigger.

### 2.3 Reward / exploit poisoning (`src/aiutopia/env/exploit.py` + `reward.py`)

`exploit.py:107` levies `NOOP_SKILL_SPAM` at `0.2 × recent_fails` per tick. With `recent_fails` capped at ~50 (the 50-tick window) → up to **−10/tick**, sustained for the duration of any "stuck in failure loop" episode. Over a 300-tick episode, the realistic worst case is **−600+**, which matches the observed `episode_return_min = −633.86`. The other penalties (DROP_SPAM `0.5 × len(recent_drops)`, OSCILLATION 0.3, LAZY_INACTION 0.2) compound.

PBRS itself is correctly formed (`reward.py:290–292`: `γΦ(s') − Φ(s)`), but **non-potential terms (DEATH, TIME, EXPLOIT, CLIP) ride alongside it without decay**. The `r_clip = 0.05 × n_clipped_param_axes` term creates a feedback loop with the unclamped Gaussian heads — as `log_std → ∞`, more axes saturate their tanh, more clip penalty, more reward poisoning.

**Effective oak vs cobble economics, accounting for everything:** the +1.000 vs +0.091 LOG_VALUE ratio is real, but exploit penalties dwarf the LOG_VALUE differential. The 80-point spread between iter-3 mean (−61.7) and max (+19.83) is *not* PPO failing to learn the oak signal — it's PPO correctly learning that the **aggregate** of (3 wandering envs at −20 each + 1 ring-farming env at +20) averages near zero, so the policy oscillates between attractors.

### 2.4 Java-side throughput collapse (`fabric_mod/src/main/java/dev/aiutopia/mod/bridge/WorldOps.java`)

`resetEpisode` clears a `33 × 5 × 33` air column above Y=66 but **does not kill item entities or XP orbs** that drop at the grass floor Y=65 — *just outside* the reset volume. Per-episode entity buildup is **linear in iterations** — the exact shape of the observed step-time degradation. The launch script uses `-Xms2g -Xmx4g -XX:+UseG1GC` with no logging; GC pauses are likely measurable but invisible.

Py4J's `advanceTickAwaitEvents` blocks on the JVM tick, which is paused by stop-the-world GC — **so a 5 s Full GC literally adds 5 s to the next measured step time**. The two effects are inseparable without GC logs.

## 3. The custom-sim question (`PROJECT_CONTEXT.md §16`)

All three agents say **YES**, build a custom Python/numpy/JAX sim for M1B, with confidence high.

**Why:**
- The M1B obs space (`spaces.py:50–84`) is entirely scalars + grids: `position`, `g_resource_grid (32×32×6)`, `g_nearest_resources (8×6)`, inventory counts. Nothing in it requires the JVM.
- The arena is *already* effectively 2D (flat grass at Y=66, log ring at Y=66). HARVEST is "is target within Manhattan distance 4.5 → set block to AIR + add to inventory" — three lines of Python.
- Throughput delta: 4 envs × ~1 step/6s now → 50k steps/s on one CPU thread in numpy = **4 orders of magnitude**.
- Sim-to-real gap risk is low *for M1B specifically* because the real env's observation is already a grid abstraction of the world.
- Bug surface shrinks dramatically — N1–N19's fix log shows ~80% of debug time was JVM/Carpet/AABB issues that simply don't exist in numpy.

**Caveats:**
- Use the same `build_role_observation_space` and `build_role_action_space` contracts so the policy is portable to real MC unchanged.
- Validate transfer via `M1EvalScenarioCallback` running against real MC every 50–100 iters.
- M2+ (builder, farmer, defender, multi-agent coordination, real chunk physics) will *not* be sim-friendly — keep the real-MC pipeline alive for those milestones.

Estimated size: **~400–500 LOC** for a fidelity-sufficient sim. Engineering cost: 1–2 days. Throughput payoff: training that currently takes 67 hours runs in **~2 minutes**.

## 5. Open decisions for the human

1. **Sim vs. real for M1B**: agents recommend sim. Do you accept the engineering cost (1–2 days) and the policy-transfer validation overhead? If yes, parallelizes nicely with applying items 1–9 to the real-MC pipeline as a backup.
2. **BC warmstart**: agents recommend it as the highest single-item ROI for the real-MC path. Acceptable to spend 2–3 hours building it before the next launch?
3. **`kl_coeff` choice**: do you want pure clip-PPO (`kl_coeff=0.0`) or fixed-KL (`kl_coeff=0.5`, `kl_target=0.02`)? The agents lean clip-only because LSTM PPO is sensitive and the adaptive runaway is what got us here.
4. **`exploit.py` philosophy**: agents recommend softening the penalties for the gatherer at stage 1. Long-term, exploits matter for multi-agent and economy stability (M3+). One option: keep the penalty *categories* but cap each category at a small per-tick magnitude and let multi-agent training in M3 expose what really needs to bite.
5. **Reset cadence vs ring size**: `WorldOps.resetEpisode` currently respawns 8 logs every ~300 ticks. Agent 3 suggests 16 logs every 150 ticks to double signal density and reduce the "ring depleted → wander" window. Worth doing alongside item 4.
